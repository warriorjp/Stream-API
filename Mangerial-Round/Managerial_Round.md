# Manager Round Interview Questions & Answers

## 1. Tell me about yourself and your journey so far.

**Answer:** I am a Java Full Stack Developer with around 6 years of
experience in Java, Spring Boot, Microservices, React, SQL, MongoDB,
Kafka, and cloud technologies. I have worked on scalable enterprise
applications, production support, stakeholder communication, and
technical leadership. I enjoy solving complex problems and continuously
learning new technologies.

## 2. How do you handle conflicts within your team?

**Answer:** I stay calm, understand both sides without taking sides,
encourage open communication, and help the team reach common ground. I
always remind everyone that we're working toward the same business goal.

## 3. Describe a time you managed a difficult stakeholder.

**Answer:** During a tight release, a stakeholder requested new features
during testing. After understanding that the request was for an
important client demo, I worked with my team to deliver only the
critical features and scheduled the remaining ones for a later release.

## 4. How do you prioritize tasks?

**Answer:** I prioritize based on business impact, urgency,
dependencies, and effort. I communicate with stakeholders to clarify
priorities, start high-impact work early, and raise risks proactively.

## 5. Tell me about a challenging project.

**Answer:** I worked on a microservices migration with strict deadlines
and production stability requirements. I coordinated with multiple
teams, planned phased releases, monitored deployments, and completed the
migration successfully with minimal production impact.

## 6. Where do you see yourself in 5 years?

**Answer:** I see myself as a strong contributor and technical leader,
continuously learning new technologies, adapting to industry changes,
and helping the organization succeed.

## 7. Why should we hire you?

**Answer:** I combine strong technical skills with ownership,
adaptability, problem-solving, and effective communication. I
consistently deliver quality solutions while supporting my team.

## 8. Why are you looking for a job change?

**Answer:** I appreciate my current organization, but I'm looking for
larger technical challenges, greater ownership, and opportunities to
continue growing professionally.

## 9. Why do you want to join our company?

**Answer:** Your technology stack, engineering culture, and business
challenges align well with my experience and long-term career goals. I'm
looking for meaningful work and continuous learning.

## 10. What are your strengths?

**Answer:** Quick learner, ownership, reliability, strong debugging
skills, adaptability, teamwork, and continuous learning.

## 11. What are your weaknesses?

**Answer:** Earlier I spent too much time perfecting small details. I've
learned to balance quality with timely delivery by focusing on business
priorities.

## 12. What will you do if your team member is not performing?

**Answer:** I'd understand the root cause, provide mentoring or
training, set clear expectations, monitor progress, and involve my
manager if improvement doesn't happen.

# Additional Questions

## 13. How do you handle production issues?

Assess impact, communicate, identify root cause, fix or rollback,
validate, and perform RCA.

## 14. Have you made a mistake?

Yes. I acknowledged it immediately, fixed it, documented the learning,
and improved my review process.

## 15. How do you handle pressure?

I stay calm, prioritize work, communicate proactively, and focus on
solving one issue at a time.

## 16. How do you motivate your team?

I encourage collaboration, appreciate good work, support teammates, and
promote open communication.

## 17. Questions to ask the interviewer

-   What are the team's biggest technical challenges?
-   What does success look like in the first six months?
-   How do you support technical learning and career growth?
-   What are the team's long-term goals?
